=Foraging=

Forageables are items that can be found and harvested in Stardew Valley. There are many different types of forageables, each with its own unique characteristics and uses.

==Seasonal Forageables==

The availability of seasonal forageables changes depending on the season. Here is a list of some common seasonal forageables:

* Spring: Apple Blossoms, Maple Syrup Sap, Morel Mushrooms
* Summer: Berries (Various Types), Cactus Fruit, Coconut
* Fall: Acorns, Apples, Gourds
* Winter: Dried Fruits and Vegetables, Mistletoe

==Common Forageables==

These forageables can be found throughout the year:

* Bamboo Shoots
* Buttercups
* Clover
* Cotton Greens
* Daisy Petals
* Ginseng
* Hazelnuts
* Honeycomb
* Mushrooms (Various Types)
* Pansies
* Ragweed
* Sagebrush
* Violets

==Special Forageables==

These forageables have special properties or uses:

* Coral: Can be found on the beach, and is used to craft certain items.
* Fiddlehead Ferns: Can be found in the jungle, and are used to craft certain items.
* Ginger: Can be found in the wild, and is used to craft certain items.
* Magma Caps: Can be found in the volcano dungeon, and are used to craft certain items.

==Foraging Experience Points==

Harvesting forageables gives experience points, which can help increase your foraging skill level. Here is a list of some common forageables and their corresponding experience point values:

* Apple Blossoms: 2 XP
* Bamboo Shoots: 3 XP
* Berries (Various Types): 1-5 XP per berry
* Buttercups: 2 XP
* Clover: 2 XP
* Cotton Greens: 3 XP
* Daisy Petals: 2 XP
* Ginseng: 4 XP
* Hazelnuts: 3 XP
* Honeycomb: 5 XP
* Mushrooms (Various Types): 1-10 XP per mushroom
* Pansies: 2 XP
* Ragweed: 2 XP
* Sagebrush: 3 XP
* Violets: 2 XP

==Foraging Tips and Tricks==

Here are some tips and tricks for foraging in Stardew Valley:

* Forage in the same spot repeatedly to increase your chances of finding rare or high-value items.
* Use your foraging skill level to determine which items you can find, and which items you cannot.
* Keep an eye out for special forageables that have unique properties or uses.
* Consider using a journal to keep track of where you've found certain forageables in the past.

==History==

Here is a list of changes made to foraging in Stardew Valley over time:

* 1.3.27: Breaking a regular tree stump now gives +1 Foraging experience.
* 1.4: Fixed bug where Foraging skill level up would cause trees to stop dropping seeds and seeds on the ground to disappear instead of being harvested until the player slept and saved the game. Changed sound when picking up forageables to a higher-pitched sound. Forester profession changed from "Wood worth 50% more" to "Trees drop 25% more wood."
* 1.6: Botanist now affects items obtained from shaking fruit trees. Shaking berry bushes now grants 1 XP per berry. Foraged items grown from wild seeds now give just 2 XP instead of 7, but also give some farming XP. Mushrooms from the Farm Cave now give 5 forage experience each. Survival Burger recipe now learned at Level 8, up from Level 2. Charcoal Kiln recipe now learned at Level 2 instead of Level 4. Cookout Kit now learned at Level 3 instead of Level 9. Tapper now learned at Level 4 instead of Level 3. Positive energy Forage can be pickled. Positive energy Forage (except Mushrooms) can be juiced. Foraged fruits and positive energy Mushrooms can be dried.
* 1.6.3: Harvesting moss grants 1 Foraging XP per moss collected.
* 1.6.12: Digging up Seed Spots and Artifact Spots, panning, harvesting ginger, and chopping twigs now grant foraging experience. Chopping trees now grants 14 XP instead of 12 and chopping stumps now grants 2 XP instead of 1.
